The first issue in the early stages was the food databases. The collections are all separate, and getting everything i think gets wildly expensive (enough so that a big business wasn't willing to buy multiple), so you have to end up picking one. This brings to the second issue, with most food tracking apps, the food labelling accuracy is the biggest issue. As the databases are limited, it means the app often has to rely on crowdsourcing the label information. Which means you end up with a load of incorrect data filling up the database, so it gets confusing and filled with multiple versions of the same foods added incorrectly (which is ultimately what made apps like MFP worse over time) We tried to mitigate this by having the crowdsourced data being submission for review only but obviously that ends up creating a huge amount of work in the backend once you scale having to sift through all the submissions and i think they gave up on it as its full of inaccurate things now. 

I don't know if i can present you with many solutions but this is a gap nobody has managed to fill so it could be something that makes it very unique if you manage to find a way around this issue.

Accuracy is probably one of THE biggest problems you will hit across multiple features. Making the system user friendly enough that people use it accurately and then also making sure the database itself is accurate.
